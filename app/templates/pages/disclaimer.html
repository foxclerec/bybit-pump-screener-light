Cryptocurrency-Specific Risks

Cryptocurrency markets carry unique and elevated risks compared to traditional financial markets:

  • Prices are extremely volatile — swings of 50% or more within hours are not uncommon.
  • Markets operate 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, with no circuit breakers or trading halts.
  • Many altcoins have low liquidity, meaning large orders can move prices dramatically and exit may be difficult or impossible at desired levels.
  • Tokens may be delisted from exchanges without warning, potentially rendering them illiquid or worthless.
  • Markets are susceptible to manipulation including pump-and-dump schemes, wash trading, and spoofing. Signals may detect manipulated price movements that typically reverse sharply.
  • Regulatory actions (enforcement, bans, asset freezes) can occur without prior notice and may affect your ability to trade or withdraw funds.
  • Cryptocurrency assets are largely unregulated in most jurisdictions and may not be protected by investor protection or deposit insurance schemes.
  • You can lose your entire investment. Many participants in cryptocurrency markets lose money.

Detection Methodology Risks

The pump detection algorithm is based on historical price pattern matching. It analyzes past price movements over configurable lookback windows and thresholds. This methodology:

  • Does not predict future price movement. A detected "pump" may reverse immediately, continue, or be a false signal.
  • Is not a validated, peer-reviewed, or regulated analytical methodology.
  • May produce false positives (alerting on non-meaningful price movements) or false negatives (missing actual pumps).
  • Performance of the detection algorithm varies with market conditions, configuration settings, and data quality.
  • Detection results depend heavily on user-configured parameters (lookback windows, thresholds, volume filters). Misconfigured settings may produce excessive false alerts or miss genuine events.
